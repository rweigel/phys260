1 Introduction – Base 10 Logarithms

5

The motivation for the base 10 logarithm is that it reduces numbers raised by a power of 10 to the power the number was raised to. So 102 becomes 2, 103 becomes 3, etc. The base 10 logarithm is sometimes called the “common logarithm”.

7

In mathematical notation,

9

log10​(10x)=x

11

For example,

13

log10​(10−5)=−5 and log10​(107)=7

15

(To take the base 10 logarithm of a number that is not exactly a power of 10, use a calculator.)

17

    29-30

    31

    33

    log10​10+log10​100=log10​10⋅100=log10​103=3

    35

  3. The difference between two logged number is the log of the ratio of the numbers:

    37-38

    39

2 Introduction – Base e Logarithm

88

The base 10 logarithm reduces numbers raised by a power of 10 to the power the number was raised to.

90

The base e logarithm reduces numbers raised by a power of e to the power the number was raised to. It is represented by loge​x, or more commonly, ln(x).

92

“ln” represents the “natural logarithm”. The term “natural” is used because the exponential e appears in many natural problems, for example, some populations grow in proportion to et/τ, where the constant τ is a growth rate.

94

In mathematical notation,

96

ln(ex)=x; for example ln(e−5)=−5 and ln(e7)=7

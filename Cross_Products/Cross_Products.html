Cross Products

2

1 Introduction

5

The cross product of two vectors v and B, written as v×B, is a vector quantity with a magnitude of ∣v∣∣B∣sinϕ=vBsinϕ, where ϕ is the angle between the two vectors (0≤ϕ≤180∘). The direction of v×B is perpendicular to the plane that contains both vectors. (There are two perpendicular directions, and the one to choose is determined using the cross–product right–hand rule; see your textbook for details.)

7

Three methods are commonly used for computing a cross product. The first method is useful for certain simple problems, whereas the last two methods apply to all problems.

9

3 By Inspection Method

62

64

1 Introduction

1

The cross product of two vectors v and B is given by v×B, which is a vector quantity with a magnitude of vBsinϕ, where ϕ is the angle between the two vectors. The direction of v×B is perpendicular to the plane that contains both vectors. (There are two perpendicular directions, and the one to choose is determined using the cross–product right–hand rule; see your textbook of this right–hand rule.)

3

There are two methods that are commonly used for computing a cross product.

5
